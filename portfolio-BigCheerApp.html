Big Cheer

As Lead Product Designer, i enjoyed a central role in developing the design strategy and supporting development of the app from concept to delivery.

Working closely with the development team, I produced detailed design proposals to create an immersive and entertaining mobile experience tailored to the US sports market, with the inclusion of ESPNU mascots and college teams.

Discovery

Big Cheer began as a side project idea from the Yarnee team, a fun idea to bring people together in a sporting crowd. I led on product design, working closely, yet remotely, with a developer to bring a whiteboard scribble through to an app store release in quick turnaround, hitting hard targets such as a release for the World Cup 2014 and the beginning on the US College Football season.

We continued to develop the app, releasing regular updates, gaining interest from the US College market and a collaboration with ESPNU to bring their branding into the app. I worked closely with the Project Lead to define branding guidelines focused toward a US sport target market, designing a new logo, interface elements and work flow which allowed for a closer connection between fans and their teams.

The Challenge - Celebrate together in a crowd with something better than a cardboard sign.

The Solution - Design an iPad app that allows multiple iPads to act as a single screen for the purpose of an animation. So whether it’s in-stadium at a game, at a tailgate event, or in front of the TV cameras, any group with iPads can put up an eye-catching sign.

Inspiration

I wanted to inject a strong sports theme through the design elements, and in particular to generate a closer connection between user and the teams they support. This brought to mind ideas of team color schemes, team specific animation elements and animation packs to collect based on the collector card packs popular from the early days of sports.

I aimed to maintain a clean, minimal design palette, both to evoke the period style i was aiming toward and to minimise the technical overhead in displaying images animating across multiple screens.

Experience

Simplicity was key here, there were to be minimal interactions to complete the primary task of displaying a 'Big Cheer'. The app was to be lightweight and efficient. Process steps were to be kept to a minimum, with the expectation that the user would be watching the sports game happening in front of them, only interacting with the app on occasion, and then in a celebratory environment with others.

The primary use case was a group of fans, in a stadium setting, supporting their team with 'cheers' at pivitol moments staggered through a game. My initial focus was on defining a streamlined workflow that ensured participating in a cheer was no more than two steps away in a standard setting.The process flow broke down into four distinct steps;

1. Select Team, 2. Define Position, 3. Select Cheer and 4. Display Cheer.

The requirement to assume no celular connection between iPads resulted in an initial study of creating a proximity-network but this was dismissed for scaleability limitations and we settled on implementing strong visual identifiers and a simple process for setting position. The assumption of a group sitting in a row provided a direct anology for displaying position cards in a row across the screen. Simply counting how many friends were to the left and right of you set your position.

Sketching

I moved quickly from sketchbook to Sketch, exploring wireframe mocks that captured the primary process steps.

Selection of the Team is likely set before the game begins and so could be considered static in the game environment, whilst the other steps are on a sliding scale of repeatability and a requirement for coordination across the group. This focused the design on a main screen which offered a single point of access for setting, and displaying, user position and quick access to a 'Cheer' library.

A cheer library was designed to accomodate sport-specific 'Cheer' packs, in the anticipation of a future implementation of a shop mechanism for purchasing animation packs.

Refinement

We pushed to deliver a version of Big Cheer branded toward the World Cup 2014 in Brazil, and so the initial release focused on supporting football (soccer) only and specifically participating teams participating, with branding closely following that of the World Cup.

Taking the app beyond this event-specific release, we refocused the app on the US sports market and expanded both the sports and teams available. The core mechanics remained consistent but the team selection and 'Cheer' library were refined to accomodate the increase in scope.

Gaining market interest, we achieved a collaboration with ESPNU to bring their mascots directly into the app. This again pushed an increase in complexity in possible options, yet inversely lead to a simplification of process flow. I redesigned the main screen to allow for direct input and selection from a library of cheers, which now displayed the complete library together with the option to partition off into 'Cheer' packs.

Results

Big Cheer released in time for each milestone and was successful in gaining interest from the market, in particular with an ESPNU collaboration that paved the way for much greater visibility in the future.

With a set of refinements and US-sports themed redesign in the pipeline there was a great opportunity to expand the success of the app. Due to other commitments for the main team, these remain waiting to be implemented.
